Refraction and Snell's Law - HKDSE Physics Summary
Refraction is the bending of light when it passes from one medium to another with 
different optical density.
Key Points:
When light travels from less dense to more dense medium (e.g., air → water, air → 
glass):
1. Refraction of Light
1.1 Definition
Light changes speed when entering different medium
Light changes direction (except at normal incidence)
Frequency remains constant
Wavelength changes

6. Dispersion of Light
6.1 Definition
6.2 Mechanism
Different colors have different wavelengths
Different wavelengths refract by different amounts
Shorter wavelength → Higher refractive index → Bends more
Longer wavelength → Lower refractive index → Bends less
6.3 Color Order in Prism
Order (least to most deviated):
Red → Orange → Yellow → Green → Blue → Indigo → Violet
Refractive index order:

6.4 Applications
Rainbow: Dispersion and reflection in water droplets
Prism: Separates white light into spectrum
Chromatic aberration: Unwanted color separation in lenses
7. Applications of Refraction
7.1 Optical Fibers
Uses total internal reflection (special case of refraction)
Transmits light signals over long distances
Used in telecommunications, internet, medical endoscopes
7.2 Lenses
Convex and concave lenses use refraction
Camera, eyeglasses, microscope, telescope
Control light path by refraction at curved surfaces
7.3 Atmospheric Refraction
Sunrise/Sunset: Sun visible before it rises/after it sets
Twinkling stars: Light refracted by moving air layers
Mirages: Hot air near ground causes refraction
