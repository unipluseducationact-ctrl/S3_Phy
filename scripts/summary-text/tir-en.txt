Total Internal Reflection - HKDSE Physics Summary
Total Internal Reflection (TIR) occurs when light traveling in a denser medium hits the 
boundary with a less dense medium at an angle greater than the critical angle, and all 
light is reflected back into the denser medium with no refraction.
For total internal reflection to occur, BOTH conditions must be satisfied:
If either condition is not met, TIR does NOT occur.
Critical angle (c) is the angle of incidence in the denser medium for which the angle of 
refraction in the less dense medium is exactly 90° (refracted ray travels along the 
boundary).

5. Applications of Total Internal Reflection
5.1 Optical Fibers
Core: High refractive index (carries light)
Cladding: Lower refractive index (surrounds core)
Light enters fiber at small angle
Hits core-cladding boundary at angle > critical angle
Total internal reflection keeps light trapped in core
Light travels long distances with minimal loss
Telecommunications (internet, phone)
Medical endoscopes (viewing inside body)
Decorative lighting
