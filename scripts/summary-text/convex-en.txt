Convex Lens - HKDSE Physics Summary
1. Basic Properties of Convex Lens
1.1 Definition and Symbol
Convex Lens = Converging Lens
Incident rays parallel to the principal axis converge at the focus after refraction
Symbol: Lens shape with outward curves on both ends (thicker in the middle)
1.2 Key Terms
Principal axis （主軸）: Horizontal line passing through the center of the lens
Optical center C （光心）: Center point of the lens
Focus F （焦點）: Point where parallel rays converge after refraction
Focal length f （焦距）: Distance from optical center to focus
2F: Point at twice the focal length from the lens
2. Three Principal Rays for Convex Lens
Rule 1: Parallel in, Focus out
A ray parallel to the principal axis passes through the focus F on the opposite side 
after refraction
Mnemonic: Parallel in, Focus out
Rule 2: Focus in, Parallel out
A ray passing through the focus F emerges parallel to the principal axis after 
refraction
Mnemonic: Focus in, Parallel out
Rule 3: Straight through center
A ray passing through the optical center C travels straight through without changing 
direction
Mnemonic: C in C out, Straight through
3. Six Image Formation Cases for Convex Lens
Case 1: Object beyond 2F
Image position: Between F and 2F on opposite side
Image nature: Real, inverted, diminished
Application: Camera, human eye
Case 2: Object at 2F
Image position: At 2F on opposite side
Image nature: Real, inverted, same size
Application: Copying machine
Case 3: Object between F and 2F
Image position: Beyond 2F on opposite side
Image nature: Real, inverted, magnified
Application: Projector, slide projector
Case 4: Object at F
Image position: At infinity
Image nature: No image formed (parallel rays after lens)
Application: Searchlight, flashlight
Case 5: Object inside F
Image position: Same side as object
Image nature: Virtual, erect, magnified
Application: Magnifying glass, reading glass
Case 6: Object at infinity
Image position: At focus F
Image nature: Real, inverted, highly diminished (point)
Application: Telescope objective lens

6. Real Image vs Virtual Image
6.1 Real Image
Formation: Actual convergence of light rays
Can be projected: Yes, can be displayed on screen
Position: Opposite side of lens from object
Orientation: Inverted
Examples: Camera image, projector image
6.2 Virtual Image
Formation: Apparent divergence of light rays (extensions intersect)
Can be projected: No, cannot be displayed on screen
Position: Same side of lens as object
Orientation: Erect
Examples: Magnifying glass image

8. Important Concepts
8.1 Relationship Between Object and Image Position
Object moves closer to lens → Image moves away from lens
Object moves away from lens → Image moves closer to lens
Object at 2F → Image at 2F (symmetry point)
