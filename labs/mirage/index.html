Why it looks like water: cooler air is denser (higher n). Hot air near asphalt is rarer (lower n). Light going from cold → hot can undergo total internal reflection when θ₁ > θc, so the sky is reflected up to your eye — like a mirror puddle.
θc = arcsin(n_hot / n_cold)
