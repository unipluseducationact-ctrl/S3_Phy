Simulation Lab

Interactive demos of how EM waves behave. Tied to your current wavelength above.

EM waves are transverse: E and B oscillate perpendicular to each other and to propagation k. Drag the 3D view to rotate.

In matter, light slows to v = c/n. Frequency stays the same, so wavelength shrinks: λmedium = λ/n.

At a boundary, the incident angle equals the reflected angle (law of reflection).

Crossing into a denser medium, the ray bends toward the normal (Snell’s law).

Waves spread after passing through narrow opening(s). With fixed slit width a (nm), longer λ diffracts more (sin θ ≈ λ/a).

Two coherent sources produce bright and dark fringes where waves arrive in or out of phase.

Different bands penetrate materials differently — from radio passing through walls to gamma rays needing thick shielding.
